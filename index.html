Tragedy Strikes Again: RFK Killed in Campaign Rally Shooting
Thursday, June 6, 1968 - By Rick Foster Kline
This Picture was Taken Mere Moments after RFk was Shot in The Ambasador Hotel In Los Angles

In a shocking and heartbreaking turn of events early this morning, Senator Robert F. Kennedy was assassinated in Los Angeles. Just after midnight on June 5, 1968, while celebrating his victory in the California Democratic primary, RFK was delivering a hopeful speech to supporters at the Ambassador Hotel. As he exited the ballroom through the kitchen pantry, a young man, later identified as Sirhan Sirhan, opened fire at close range, fatally wounding the senator. Kennedy was rushed to the hospital, but despite desperate efforts by doctors, he succumbed to his injuries. Crowds have gathered outside the hospital, and an overwhelming sense of grief is spreading across the nation as Americans grapple with the devastating news. RFK, seen by many as a beacon of hope and a champion for civil rights, poverty alleviation, and peace, is mourned by millions.

RFK’s assassination is already drawing immediate comparisons to the murder of his brother, President John F. Kennedy, just five years ago. The 42-year-old senator was widely expected to challenge Vice President Hubert Humphrey for the Democratic nomination and had run a campaign centered on equality, unity, and an end to the Vietnam War. Authorities have taken Sirhan Sirhan into custody, and investigations are underway to determine his motives. Civil rights leaders, activists, and political figures are speaking out, expressing both their grief and their concerns about the state of the country. For many, RFK’s death feels like the loss of a guiding voice, leaving a nation already fractured by violence and conflict with a renewed sense of fear and uncertainty about the future.
